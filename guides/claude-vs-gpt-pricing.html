Claude vs GPT: API Pricing Compared

Both companies ship a budget, mid-tier, and flagship model. Here's exactly what each one costs per million tokens, head to head, plus every other major provider for context.

Prices below are a snapshot as of July 8, 2026. For always-current numbers, use the live calculator.

Claude vs GPT, tier by tier

The two companies structure their lineups almost identically: a cheap, fast model for high-volume simple tasks, a balanced mid-tier model most production apps actually use, and a flagship reasoning model for the hardest problems. Comparing within the same tier is a more useful exercise than comparing flagships alone, since most real applications live in the budget or mid tier.

At the budget tier, GPT-4.1 mini is the cheaper of the two. At mid-tier, Claude Sonnet 5's introductory pricing currently undercuts GPT-5.4 on output cost. At flagship, the two are close on input price, GPT-5.5 runs higher on output. None of this settles which model is "better," capability varies by task, but for cost alone, the gap is usually smaller than people assume, the real cost driver tends to be which tier you pick, not which company you pick.

The number that actually decides your bill

A price-per-million-tokens table only tells half the story. Your actual monthly cost depends on how many tokens your specific prompts and responses use, and that varies a lot by use case. A model that's cheaper per token can still cost you more overall if it tends to write longer responses, or if your prompts are much larger than average.
